Group Processes

TeamTekkerz worked reasonably well together for A2, but we identified a few places where we would need necessary improvements to do better on this assignment.
The first change was more communication, especially through Microsoft Teams. We weren’t really keeping each other informed about where we were at with our designated work, and it was difficult to get in touch when we needed to ask questions of specific individuals.
As such, we have decided as a collective to be more active on Teams and also try to make it to meetings. We all have busy schedules at the moment, so if someone isn’t able to make it to a meeting, they are expected to check the meeting agenda for to-do items and then check in on Teams.
Another element we want to change is the distribution of work. Making sure that everyone has a more equal share of the content creation.

Career Plans

All of us have kept to the same career plans we chose in A2. We have all got similar goals, in that majority of the roles require knowledge of coding languages, teamwork and excellent communication skills.
Most of us have chosen fields where we will be working alongside other IT professionals, and will be required to contribute our share to an overall larger project.
The roles however do differentiate within the details of the job descriptions. The expected pay is varied, from the lower end of $59,000, to the higher end of $88,000.
This of course reflects the responsibilities and leadership of the roles, with the higher paid salaries expected to manage a group, or showcase more specialised skill or knowledge.
Some of us will require more design knowledge than others, and some will be expected to work closely alongside their clients throughout the process.
Qualifications are also broad, with most of us needing at least a bachelor’s degree at University, plus also a more specialised course relating to our chosen field.
Overall, although we may have a common starting point, our skill-sets will be quite diverse by the time we reach our goals.
